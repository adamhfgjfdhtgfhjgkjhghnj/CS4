Legal & Intellectual Property

Intellectual property laws protect creators by preventing unauthorized copying of software and content. However, they can also limit innovation because developers may not be able to freely build on existing work. Open source licenses allow more freedom, while proprietary licenses are more restrictive.

Privacy Concerns

Modern systems collect data automatically through apps and websites. This includes location, searches, and user behavior. Many users are not fully aware of how much data is being generated and stored.

This raises concerns about who controls the data and how it is used, especially when sensitive information is involved.

Social, Economic & Cultural Impact

Social

Technology changes communication and social behavior.

Economic

Companies benefit from user data, but privacy may be reduced.

Cultural

Computing connects global cultures but can also change traditions.

Environmental

E-waste and energy use affect the environment.

Threats & Protection

Malware

Viruses, spyware, and ransomware can steal or damage data.

Phishing

Fake messages trick users into giving personal information.

Protection

Use strong passwords, updates, and antivirus tools.

Encryption

Protects data so only authorized users can access it.

Software Licenses

Software licenses control how programs can be used and shared. Proprietary licenses restrict use, while open source licenses allow modification and distribution.

Choosing the right license depends on fairness, purpose, and how much freedom users need.
